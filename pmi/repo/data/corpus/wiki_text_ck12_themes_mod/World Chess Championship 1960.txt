The 1960 World Chess Championship was played between Mikhail Botvinnik and Mikhail Tal in Moscow from March 15 to May 7, 1960. Tal won. An interzonal chess tournament was held in Portoro, Slovenia (then Yugoslavia) in August and September 1958. The top six finishers qualified for the Candidates tournament. The 1959 Candidates Tournament was held in Yugoslavia in Bled, Zagreb, and Belgrade. The top two players from the previous tournament, Smyslov and Keres, were seeded directly into the tournament and joined by the top six from the interzonal. Mikhail Tal won, becoming the challenger in the 1960 championship match. The best of 24 game match was held in Moscow. In the event of a 1212 tie, Botvinnik, the title holder, would retain the Championship. Tal won.
