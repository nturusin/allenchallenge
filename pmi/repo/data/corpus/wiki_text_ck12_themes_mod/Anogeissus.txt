Anogeissus is a genus of trees in the family Combretaceae. There are about 10 to 12 species distributed in Asia and tropical Africa. These are trees and shrubs with tubular flowers that lack petals and dry fruits with ridges or wings. Axlewood (A. latifolia) is used for its wood and tannins and as a fodder. African birch (A. leiocarpa) is used for its wood and to make yellow dye and medicinal compounds. Species include: Anogeissus acuminata Anogeissus bentii Anogeissus dhofarica Anogeissus latifolia Anogeissus leiocarpus Anogeissus rotundifolia Anogeissus schimperi Anogeissus sericea
