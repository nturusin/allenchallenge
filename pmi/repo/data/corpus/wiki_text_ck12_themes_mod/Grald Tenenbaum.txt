Grald Tenenbaum is a French mathematician and novelist, born in Nancy, France on 1 April 1952. An alumnus of the cole Polytechnique, he has been professor of mathematics at the Institut lie Cartan at Henri Poincar University (Nancy-1) since 1981. An associate of Paul Erds and specialist in analytic and probabilistic number theory, Grald Tenenbaum received the A-X Gaston Julia prize in 1976, the Albert Chtelet medal in algebra and number theory in 1985 and, together with Michel Mends France, the Paul-mile Doistau Blutet prize from the French Academy of Sciences in 1999. His novel L'Ordre des jours, published in 2008 by Hlose d'Ormesson, received the Erckmann-Chatrian prize the same year. (with Richard R.
