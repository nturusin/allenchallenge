Rick Schwartz (born 1967) is an American film and television producer and financier based in New York, whose credits include: The Departed, Black Swan, Gangs of New York, The Aviator, Machete, and The Others. Raised in Teaneck, New Jersey, Schwartz grew up in a Modern Orthodox Jewish home and graduated from the Moriah School and the Frisch School. He has been a resident of Englewood, New Jersey. Schwartz began his film career at Miramax under Harvey and Bob Weinstein, working there for seven years and eventually serving as the company's senior vice president of production. At Miramax, Schwartz oversaw the development and production of projects including Martin Scorseses Gangs of New York, The Aviator, and The Departed, and Alejandro Amenabars The Others. From 2008 to 2014, Schwartz was CEO and Founder of Overnight Productions, which has financed and produced movies by directors including Robert Rodriguez, Darren Aronofsky and Gela Babluani. In 2014, Schwartz and Jimmy Fallon partnered in Eight Million Plus Productions, a New York-based production company. Current shows include Spike TV's Lip Sync Battle and NBC's Sharing. Schwartz is Chief Strategic Officer for Lua Technologies. Schwartz is active in numerous charities, including The Robin Hood Foundation, United Jewish Appeal, and Amfar.
