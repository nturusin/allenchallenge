Milev Rocks (Bulgarian: , Milevi Skali \'mi-le-vi ska-'li\) is the group of rocks off the north coast of Robert Island in the South Shetland Islands, Antarctica, situated east of Henfield Rock, south-southwest of Orsoya Rocks and southwest of Mellona Rocks, and extending 1.5 km (0.93 mi) in east-west direction and 600 m (656 yd) in north-south direction. The rocks are named after the Bulgarian poet Geo Milev (pseudonym of Georgi Milyov Kasabov, 18951925). Milev Rocks are centred at 621846S 593254W, which is 3.06 km (1.90 mi) north-northwest of Newell Point. British mapping in 1968 and Bulgarian in 2009.
