The Transport Department of the Government of Hong Kong is a department of the civil service responsible for transportation-related policy in Hong Kong. The department is under the Transport and Housing Bureau. The Transport Department was created in 1968 as a separate department within the Hong Kong Government. Prior to 1968 it was assigned to the Transport Office under the Colonial Secretary's department. registration of vehicles in Hong Kong licensing of rail and bus operators in Hong Kong licensing offices all roads within Hong Kong co-ownership of bridges and tunnels (mainly a public-private mix) 18,000 on-street metered parking spaces The post of Commissioner for Transport is currently held by Ingrid Yeung, and was previously held by Joseph Lai. The Commissioner reports to the Secretary for Transport and Housing.
