The Tetraviridae are a family of viruses so named because they have a T=4 symmetry (T refers to the triangulation number), are extremely host specific, and infect moths and butterflies of the following genera: Genus Betatetravirus; type species: Nudaurelia capensis virus (see Nudaurelia cytherea capensis) Genus Omegatetravirus; type species: Nudaurelia capensis virus This family was divided into three families by the ICTV in 2011: Alphatetraviridae, Carmotetraviridae and Permutotetraviridae.
