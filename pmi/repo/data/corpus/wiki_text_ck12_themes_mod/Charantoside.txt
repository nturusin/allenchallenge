Charantoside is any of several related cucurbitane triterpenoid glycosides found in the fruits bitter melon vine (Momordica charantia). They include: charantoside I, (19R,23E)-5,19-Epoxy-19-methoxycucurbita-6,23,25-trien-3-ol 3-O--D-glucopyranoside: amorphous solid. charantoside II, (19R,23R)-5,19-Epoxy-19,23-dimethoxycucurbita-6,24-dien-3-ol 3-O--D-allopyranoside: amorphous solid. charantoside III, (23E)-5,19-Epoxycucurbita-6,23,25-trien-3-ol 3-O--D-glucopyranoside: amorphous solid. charantoside IV, (23E)-5,19-Epoxycucurbita-6,23,25-trien-3-ol 3-O--D-allopyranoside: colorless needles, melting at 256260 C. charantoside V, (23R)-5,19-Epoxy-23-methoxycucurbita-6,24-dien-3-ol 3-O--D-glucopyranoside: colorless needles, melting at 235240 C. charantoside VI, (23S)-5,19-Epoxy-23-methoxycucurbita-6,24-dien-3-ol 3-O--D-allopyranoside: amorphous solid. charantoside VII, (23E)-3-Hydroxycucurbita-6,23,25-trien-5,19-olide 3-O--Dglucopyranoside: colorless needles, melting at 258262 C charantoside VIII, (23E)-3-Hydroxy-7,25-dimethoxycucurbita-5,23-dien-19-al 3-O--D-glucopyranoside: amorphous solid. Charantosides I through VIII can be extracted from the fresh fruit with methanol and ethyl acetate.
